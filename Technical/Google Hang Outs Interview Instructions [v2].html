Preparing Your Environment for the Interview:

Here are some helpful hints for getting the best possible video interview on Google Hangouts

Try to put your camera at eye level.

If you are using a camera built into a laptop computer, try to raise the laptop so that the camera is as close to eye level as possible.

Use earbuds or earphones during the interview

Often, the sound from your machine's speakers is picked up by your machine's microphone which creates annoying echos in the recording. If possible, plan on plugging earphones/earbuds into your machines speaker jack and wear the earbuds during the interview. Often you can arrange the wires of the earbuds so they are not noticeable.

Get close to the camera

The general rule of thumb is that you want to be close enough to the camera that you can see the whites of your eyes in the picture. (But not _too_ close)

Check your background

Look at the background of your video shot and look for problems. Is there any glare of mirrors, glass, or lights that can be eliminated? Does it look like there is a plant or lamp growing out of your head? Is there motion in the background that will distract the viewer?

Check your audio levels

When speaking into your microphone during a Google Hangout, there will be an audio level indicator in the upper right corner of the page represented by five dots that flashes green, yellow and red to indicate your audio level> the best sound level is achieved when all three green dots and the yellow dot light up and sometimes the red dot lights up. You can experiment to see how loud you have to speak to achieve these sound levels.

Improve the audio quality

Usually there is not much you can do to improve the quality of the audio in a typical office or home environment. But if there is too much room noise or echo in your audio, you can try to mitigate it by putting a curtain behind you or put soft things on the walls to absorb the room echo.

During The Interview

Your interview is not a live interview. It is being recorded and will be editing into a future episode of This Week On developerWorks. This means that we can reshoot any part of the interview that you don't feel good about. So if Calvin asks you a question and you fumble on answering, just feel free to stop, pause a 2 or 3 seconds, and then start over. Calvin will edit the interview recording so that it only contains your good responses.

Please try not to read a script for your answers. Instead it's best to memorize about three bullet points for each question and just tal to the camera about them.
